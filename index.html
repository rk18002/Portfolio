Color Detection

Color detection projects leverage the power of computer vision to discern and categorize colors within digital media, such as images and video feeds. At the core of these projects lies the transformation of color representations, often shifting from the standard RGB (Red, Green, Blue) format to HSV (Hue, Saturation, Value). This conversion facilitates easier color isolation, as HSV separates color information into distinct components. Subsequently, specific color ranges are defined, allowing for the creation of masks that isolate desired hues. These masks, essentially binary images, highlight regions matching the target colors.
